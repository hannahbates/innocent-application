about me

If I were on blind date and got asked "which fruit would you be and why?" I'd say "Well Cilla, I'd have to be a cucumber because they are cool, not the obvious choice of fruit and they are great with a gin and tonic (on Friday nights)."

To give my application a bit of zest I wanted to present it in this special way. I just wanted to add pictures really. And incorporate batman if at all possible.

i took the initiative and made something happen...

My passion for making things better began when I reorganised a stationary cupboard at Topshop aged 18. I was a local hero. We discovered stationary we never knew existed and a never ending supply of those "M" cubes for the hangers.

This excitement went from strength to strength and last year my current manager and I knew our company were missing out big time by not having an in-house group sales department. We were using an external company who we're taking 10% that could have been ours. After a bit of prodding-the-big-bosses we presented our new branding and ideas for marketing it. We got given a budget of £5,000 to spend in return for a promise we'd take a certain amount in 6 months. We took ten times what they'd asked. By nurturing new contacts (creating colourful spread sheets) and inviting them to see new shows first we made a lot of friends which was really fun and exciting every time they called with another coach load of theatre enthusiasts. We also helped initiate a teachers preview club to encourage new audiences. It was really thrilling calling teachers by their first name!

why do i want to work for innocent?

To put it simply, why wouldn't you want to? When your heart skips a beat when veg pots are on BOGOF, when you go and visit your mum and she's brought innocent cartons for kids (i.e. me, her 26 year old daughter) and your face shines brighter than the refrigerator's light you know you're onto a winner. Any organisation who gives me something to read and ponder whilst I'm slurping my smoothie is instantly something I want to be part of. You knit hats for smoothies for goodness sakes!

On a serious note, I think this role could be the apple to my raspberry. The fact that I'm having the best time writing this application tells me a lot of what I need to know. On a day to day basis it's the customer service that I get a kick out of. At the moment I'm communicating by email and telephone all day with the public and other ticketing agencies; sending invoices, solving problems and providing information from restricted views to whether the show will contain loud bangs. I love helping people in a sticky situation, or giving them some insider information to make their experience the best I can make it. I pride myself on the notion that "people" are what I do best, so to speak.

Your investment in people, commitment to your staff, social responsibilities and the ways in which you go about that are so unique and that's something I could kill (kill meaning juice an apple) to be a part of and would equally want to give everything to.

Also Hannah* rhymes with banana.

i had lots on but still smiled when...

Well firstly you should know that in 1994 I won a packet of Chewits for being the best smiler whilst doing a tap routine dressed as Shirley temple so smiling has since come extremely naturally to me. I now have a total of 9 fillings and a phobia of the dentist.

I work in a small team of three and recently I lost one member to San Francisco and then my boss to long term sickness. Three people's work became mine, all mine. Some folk swooped in to help me cover the work load but I had to train these new heros on quite a complex ticketing programme whilst fulfilling the departments needs. I felt slightly hysterical for a total of ten minutes until I got handed some cake. Then, like a scene from Cinderella, the post-its became my best friends and I had those flags and ticks cleaning up my inbox for me. The words "okie dokie," "no worries" and "wonderful, right, next..." fell out of my mouth like a never ending smoothie-fall. I really enjoyed working with the new team and considering their needs in this transition then feeling like a proud parent after a couple of weeks when they'd settled into their new roles. In terms of prioritising and managing time I can safely say I have learnt a lot about that in my current role over the last year. Theatre can be extremely dramatic, I'll leave it to your imagination for now...
